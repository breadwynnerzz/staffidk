# Internal Conduct

Internal staff conduct is just as important as public conduct. Staff channels, meetings, evaluations, and leadership discussions must remain respectful and organized.

## Internal Standards

- Respect all staff members regardless of rank
- Keep staff-only information private
- Do not create cliques or internal drama
- Do not undermine leadership decisions publicly
- Bring concerns through the correct chain of command
- Accept correction professionally

## Staff Information Security

Staff discussions, punishment logs, tickets, evidence, and internal decisions should not be shared outside approved staff areas.

> [!DANGER]
> Leaking internal staff information may result in immediate removal.

## Internal Disagreement

Disagreements should be handled privately, calmly, and through leadership. Staff members may question procedures respectfully, but should not create public arguments or staff division.
